The Pragmatic Adopter
"AI is a tool — powerful, but manageable with the right habits."
n=10 · 21% of sample · Daily users
Thought process model
This group integrates AI deeply into daily life but maintains a critical, deliberate mindset. They verify frequently, acknowledge risks rationally, and believe both companies and users share responsibility. Their lower emotional alarm — despite strong regulation support — suggests a systems-thinking approach: "fix the rules, then trust the tool."
It should follow guidelines that don't interfere with the decision making of a human.
AI should prioritise human well-being, fairness and transparency, avoiding harm, bias and manipulation.

The Ethical Advocate
"AI is powerful and that's exactly why it needs accountability."
n=23 · 48% of sample · Largest group
Thought process model
The dominant mindset. These respondents are engaged, informed AI users who have internalised societal risks — misinformation, bias, IP theft, environmental cost. They use AI heavily yet push hard for systemic accountability. Their thought process combines utility-maximising behaviour with moral vigilance: "I use it, I benefit, but I hold it responsible."
Privacy protection, fairness, accountability.
AI content should clearly show it's generated by AI. Companies must follow strict environmental guidelines.
There is not much we can do as end users except cross-verify and fact-check AI.

The Cautious Skeptic
"I use it when I need to, but I'm not convinced the fuss is warranted."
n=12 · 25% of sample
Thought process model
This group acknowledges AI risks but feels no urgency to act — either personally or via policy. Their lower verification frequency, resistance to strict regulation, and emphasis on freedom-over-safety reflect a libertarian-leaning mindset: "Acknowledge the problem, don't over-correct." They trust themselves to navigate AI risks over institutional guardrails.
Privacy, prevention of misinformation and unbiased answers.
Guidelines should prioritise freedom of speech; companies shouldn't over-censor AI if it limits creativity.
Transparency and privacy.

The Resistor
"The risks outweigh the benefits. We never needed this."
n=3 · 6% of sample · Rarely/never use AI
Thought process model
The smallest but most ideologically coherent group. Their near-total rejection of AI stems from a deeply held belief that the technology is net-harmful. They see AI as a systemic threat to truth, creative labour, and the environment. Their thought model is precautionary and rights-based: "When in doubt, protect — and when not in doubt, ban."
AI must refuse any content that promotes hate speech or discrimination against minority groups.
AI-generated misinformation should be treated like libel. If an AI hallucinates a lie about a real person, the company should be held accountable.
We should completely ban AI — life was just fine without it, if not better.
